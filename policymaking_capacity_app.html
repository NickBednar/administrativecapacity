Policymaking Capacity
in the Federal Bureaucracy

An interactive index of administrative capacity across departments and bureaus of the United States government, observed annually from 1998 through 2021.

The capacity score summarizes the policymaking competence of a federal agency or bureau in a given year, derived from a battery of organizational and personnel indicators. Higher values indicate greater capacity; the score is centered roughly on zero, with most agencies falling between −2 and +3.

Use the year strip below to scrub through observations. Click any agency in the rankings to add its trajectory to the chart at right; click again to remove. Toggle between Large Agencies — cabinet departments and major independent agencies — and Bureaus, the sub-units within them.
